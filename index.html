On September 15th, 2021, the Washington Post reported that COVID-19 killed 1 in 500 Americans. 1 in 500 However, the pandemic has not touched American lives uniformly. Demographics have played a large role.
A visual remembrance of 1 in 500 deaths in real time
COVID-19s disproportionate effect on older Americans
1 in 500 deaths translates to approximately 49 deaths each hour since the first death from COVID-19 was recorded on February 29th, 2020.
The visualization runs for one hour. Each circle represents a person who died from COVID-19 in an average hour.
The duration of time it takes for a circle to travel from its starting place to its final resting place represents the frequency at which people from each age group have died throughout the pandemic. Older people represent a disproportionate number of deaths compared with younger people.
At the end of the average hour, 49 people have passed from COVID-19.
