a visual remembrance of covid-19 deaths in real time

The disproportionate effect of COVID-19 on older Americans

On September 15th, 2021, the Washington Post reported that throughout the pandemic, 1 in 500 Americans died from COVID-19. This visualization attempts to capture this devastating number through the use of time. 1 in 500 deaths translates to approximately 49 deaths each hour since the first death from COVID-19 was recorded on February 29th, 2020.

The visualization runs for one hour. Each circle represents a person who died from COVID-19 in an average hour.

The duration of time it takes for a circle to travel from its starting place to its final resting place represents the frequency at which people from each age group died throughout the pandemic. Older people represent a disproportionate number of deaths compared with younger people.
